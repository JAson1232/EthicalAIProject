RECAP

Each context (healthcare, social media, work, tax) has different norms about
who can access what information and for what purpose.
In the case of the tax authorities, they don’t give you a choice over what
information you will share
Privacy violations occur when these norms are broken, even if information is
technically public. This accounts for modern digital data practices better than
older “secrecy” models
Problems: Sounds a bit conservative, does not tell us what is then
appropriate. What counts as context? Who decides what is appropriate? How
can we make sure information from one context (medical records) does not
leak into other contexts (healthy insurance/work)?
